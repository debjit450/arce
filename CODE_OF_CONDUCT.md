# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We want ARCE to be a professional, respectful project for contributors and users. Participation in this repository means agreeing to engage constructively and respectfully.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contribute to a positive environment:

- Giving and receiving technical feedback in good faith
- Focusing discussion on code, behavior, and design tradeoffs
- Being respectful of different experience levels and viewpoints
- Accepting responsibility for mistakes and fixing them quickly

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- Harassment, insults, or personal attacks
- Trolling, deliberately inflammatory comments, or bad-faith arguments
- Publishing private information without permission
- Repeatedly derailing technical discussions

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Repository maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing this code of conduct. They may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, or other contributions that are not aligned with these standards.

## Scope

This code of conduct applies within repository spaces and in public spaces when an individual is representing the project.

## Enforcement

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, contact the maintainers through the repository's GitHub contact channels. Reports will be reviewed and handled as promptly and fairly as possible.
